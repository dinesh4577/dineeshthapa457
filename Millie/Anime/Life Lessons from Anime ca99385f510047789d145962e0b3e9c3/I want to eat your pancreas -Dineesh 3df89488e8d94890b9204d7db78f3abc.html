“Me” and Sakura

I Want to Eat Your Pancreas is not a zombie, action movie. I became aware of this when I was handed a box of tissues upon entering the theater. Based on the award-winning novel by Yoru Sumino, I Want to Eat your Pancreas is about two high schoolers on different sides of the social spectrum. One character is dying and wants to live and the other is alive but is dead inside.

Sakura is sick with pancreatic disease and forces her friendship on a boy who she discovers reading the diary she lost in the hospital lobby. The interesting thing about this boy is that he doesn’t reveal his name to her until they go on a trip together. Sure enough this doesn’t bother Sakura. For someone who is “Living with Dying” she finds some comfort with someone who turns out to be distant from people and goes out of his way to not reveal too much emotion or become attached.

The story does not involve many other people and in our opinion that is a good thing. Everything is focused around our unnamed male character and his struggles with understanding his emotions and why he is drawn to Sakura. Her energy scares him but he is also drawn to her. Drawn to her desire to live a normal life, something which he himself feels he is struggling with. The overall message from the story seems to be to appreciate the time you spend with people, because it can all be gone at a moments notice. It is almost sad to watch each adventure they go on together, knowing what the future hold for Sakura, but it makes you enjoy her presence that much more whenever she is on screen. I Want to Eat Your Pancreas touches many emotions, happy and sad. It gives lessons in life that sometimes we often forget and it teaches us that even in the saddest moments we can still enjoy life.

Lesson #1

We can die any time. Whether we are battling a disease or are perfectly healthy. It’s important to live your life to the fullest, and live it how you would normally. As I stated before, you can be perfectly healthy today, and get into an unexpected accident the next. This was the common theme I could see strung along in the movie. From what Sakura says at the start, and what happens at the end. She essentially reveals what will happen before it does, and if you pay attention to the tv throughout, you’ll notice large foreshadowing early on. This is very hard to explain without spoiling anyone who reads this, so bear with me. The movie also conveys how much someone can change your life, we see our main character completely change from start to end of this movie. A lot of people were unhappy with the ending, I find it brilliant. I think the author displayed something that couldn’t be done with a light hearted ending. I know I repeated myself and didn’t go into much detail, but I hope you get what I’m trying to say.

Her death was just about the fact that anyone could die at anytime.

Review

I want to eat your pancreas or Kimi no Suizō o Tabetai (Japanese: 君の膵臓をたべたい)also known as Let me eat your pancreas is a Japanese anime movie, based on a novel written by Yoru Sumino. The anime movie directed by Shin'ichirō Ushijima.

Plot: The story revolves around a girl, Sakura Yamauchi, who suffers a fatal pancreatic disease, who came across an aloof boy in a hospital waiting room one day. Soon the boy discovers about her secret disease. They both study in the same class but never talked with each other. After the following incident, the girl felt comfortable around him because he was the only one who knew the truth about her and she could openly speak of her thoughts. Will anyone come to know about her disease? Will she be able to recover from her disease and survive?

Pros: First of all, the catchy title. My first reason of watching this anime was this title. I thought it is related to some vampire and all but that's absolutely false. The scenes are set in the month of April due to which the background scenery is quite beautiful amd goes very well with the anime. I personally liked the ending very much.

Cons: Few side character could have been given more importance,like Sakura's best friend, who eventually became the main character of the story at the end. I expected a little more explanation of her character like her past. The dub is really very bad.
