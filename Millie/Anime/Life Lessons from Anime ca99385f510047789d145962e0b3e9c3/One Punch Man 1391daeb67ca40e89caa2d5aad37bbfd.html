Well, this is going to be depressing.

The life lessons are:

  • “In the end, aside from few close friends nobody will know who you truly are and what you’re capable of. ”
  • “Your efforts will go unrecognized or will be attributed to another. Should you achieve something great people can call you a fraud, and even those you saved will not be grateful”.

THAT is saitama’s story. It is not uplifting, if you think about it.

Something like that.

REVIEW

One-Punch Man is one of the most-well crafted anime/manga stories out there right now. To answer the question, I will address each of the reasons why I think so in sections:

1. Satire

This is the same reason we enjoy Weird Al Yankovic and the Daily Show. Specifically, we enjoy well-executed satire. The series throws every superhero convention and storytelling formula right out of the window and asks the question :

What if your hero was a God?

Whilst one may argue that Superman and Thor are other great attempts to answer this question, One-Punch Man takes a noticeably different route. Unlike Superman who seeks acceptance, or Thor, who believe in a mandate to protect his realm, Saitama just does it for fun, a fact he reiterates numerous times throughout the series.

This is absolutely refreshing for anyone who grew up being all too familiar with the multitude of superheroes or anime characters. Somewhere in our minds, we realise we are getting bored with the archenemies and the "great power great responsibility mantras". 3. Deconstruction

I know, I know. This term has been abused one too many times. Whilst the meaning may vary, I am going with this definition (and I hope most people would agree with me) :

Deconstruction is taking some element of story-telling (ie. Superheroes) and ask : if this happened in real-life, what are the consequences?

Whilst the story started completely outlandish, the story progressed to include The Heroes Association and its Class System. This is a bureaucratic system designed to govern and control superheroes, assigning them a specific rank depending on their skill and/or popularity. Something like this would undoubtedly exist in our world, should superheroes suddenly appear. As a consequence of such a system, the heroes becomes jaded and self-serving, inculcating an elitist attitude amongst the higher ranking members of the association. Some of the notable consequences are:

The “tradition” of newbie crushing

Rank Elitism

Although these are not very "heroic" qualities, they are very human flaws that are present in any bureaucratic system. The series explore this in surprising depth, giving an interesting deconstruction of the superhero archetype.

3. ReconstructionSometimes, the deconstruction of an element also allows for a reconstruction. A Reconstruction accepts the criticisms of the initial fantasy made in deconstruction then modifies it into something that would resemble the original trope. This is exemplified by Saitama perfectly. Why? You have this system that is so bogged down by politics and bureaucracy. A system that makes heroes who value their own popularity and ranking over being noble.

Then Saitama appears.

This is a man who transcends the system, not giving a damn about its inner workings or the politics. He simply does what he wants simply cause he wants to be a hero, giving him a much stronger moral fibre when compared to the other jaded heroes, thereby reconstructing the hero archetype. This quote by him is one of the highlights of the series and it is the quintessence of how Saitama is an example of the reconstruction

I believe this is another reason why people love the series. You laugh at the hilarity of his antics and his issues. But he is an absolutely relatable person, despite being so ridiculously overpowered. He worries about rent, food and sales. To him, fighting monsters is just a hobby. Sometimes its almost a chore. But he does it anyway. Cause he is a hero. By contrasting him with characters like Genos( who is driven by revenge) and other heroes in the association, Saitama becomes incredibly likeable, not only because he is like an average man, he is what we aspire to be : a true hero.

I wont go so far to say that this anime is the best anime ever concocted by the human mind. Nor is it without flaws. But I have no doubt it will go down as one of those animes that we will remember as a classic. Because in world where cliches and recycled formulae are abundant, its nice to see something refreshingly funny and gorgeous to watch.
